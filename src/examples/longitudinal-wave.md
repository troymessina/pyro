# Traveling Longitudinal Wave

A medium of particles with a traveling longitudinal wave.

## What You'll See

- A gridded medium of particles shifting left and right with compression and rarefaction demonstrated by particle color
- The particles comprising the medium of the wave does not travel with the wave
- The wavelength \(\lambda\) is shown with a double-arrow and label

## Physics

- **Traveling Wave** \( y(x, t) = A \sin(kx-\omega t) \)
- **Wavenumber** \(k = 2\pi/\lambda\)
- **Angular Frequency** \(\omega=2\pi/T=2\pi f\)

## Key Parameters

| Parameter | Value | Description |
|:-----------|:-------:|-------------:|
| Amplitude (A) | \( 0.5~{\rm m} \) | Height of wave |
| Wavelength (\(\lambda\)) | \( 8~{\rm m} \) | Length of wave repetition |
| Wavenumber (k) | \( 2\pi/\lambda~{\rm m^{-1}}\) | Inverse wavelength or energy |
| Angular frequency (\(\omega\)) | \( 2\pi~{\rm rad/s}\) | Repetition rate of wave |

## Tips

- Drag the view to rotate and see the wave from different angles
- Change `amplitude`, `wavelength`, or `frequency` to observe changes in behavior
